UNIT SEVEN: NUTRITIONAL
DISORDERS
I. Vitamin Deficiencies
1. Nutritional Rickets:
Description:  Vitamin D deficiency causing impaired calcium and phosphorus absorption
Risk factors:  dark skin, infancy, prematurity, lack of sunlight, winter season
Signs:  enlarged head, delayed fontanel closure, soft skull bones, pigeon chest, rachitic rosary, spinal
deformities, bowed legs, muscle weakness
Diagnosis:  X-rays, serum phosphorus (low), alkaline phosphatase (high)
Prevention:  adequate  sunlight,  calcium,  phosphorus,  vitamin  D  supplementation  (400  IU/day  for
breastfed infants)
Treatment:  oral vitamin D (1500-5000 IU/day), IM injections if resistant, calcium supplementation
Nursing:  gentle handling, avoid pressure on bones, loose diapers
2. Infantile Tetany:
Description:  Neuromuscular irritability due to low ionized calcium
Signs:  muscle spasms, Trousseau’s sign, laryngospasm, cyanosis
Treatment:  IV calcium gluconate, diazepam for convulsions, magnesium sulfate if resistant
3. Scurvy:
Description:  Vitamin C deficiency
Signs:  pseudoparalysis, irritability, anemia, bleeding gums, petechiae
Treatment:  oral or parenteral vitamin C
Nursing:  gentle handling, infection prevention, mouth care, careful diapering•

II. Malnutrition Diseases
1. Marasmus:
Description:  Severe deficiency of calories and protein
Causes:  inadequate  milk  intake,  prematurity,  congenital  anomalies,  metabolic/endocrine  disorders,
infections, poor socioeconomic conditions
Signs:  growth retardation, severe muscle wasting, loss of subcutaneous fat, “senile face”, hypothermia,
anemia
Treatment:  high calorie diet, infection control, fluid therapy, warmth, milk feeding
Prevention:  education on nutrition and breastfeeding, early treatment of infections
2. Kwashiorkor:
Description:  Protein deficiency with adequate calories
Causes:  unbalanced diet, faulty feeding after marasmus, psychological factors, infections
Signs:  edema, growth failure, muscle wasting, mental apathy, hair and skin changes, anemia, vitamin
deficiencies
Treatment  follows  WHO  10-step  protocol:  including  hypoglycemia,  hypothermia,  dehydration,
electrolyte management, infection control, cautious feeding, catch-up growth, stimulation, and family
education
Prevention:  balanced diet, breastfeeding, early detection and treatment
3. Marasmic-Kwashiorkor:
Description:  Combination of marasmus and kwashiorkor features, severe wasting with edema
Failure to Thrive (FTT)
Definition:  Inadequate weight gain and growth below 3rd-5th percentile for age.
Types:
Organic:  due to medical causes
Nonorganic:  due to inadequate intake/environmental factors
Mixed:  combination of both
Signs:  Poor growth, feeding difficulties, developmental delay, irritability•
