Leukemia
Definition:
Malignant neoplasm of blood-forming organs, characterized by overproduction and
accumulation of immature white blood cells, reducing normal blood components. 
Types:
Acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL): most common in children aged 2-6 years
Acute myeloid leukemia (AML): 20% of childhood leukemia, more common in adolescence
Pathogenesis:
Rapid multiplication of immature WBCs, decreased normal blood cell production, leading to
anemia, bleeding, infection risk, organomegaly.
Clinical Manifestations:
Pallor, petechiae, anorexia, vomiting, weight loss, fever, tachycardia, dyspnea, abdominal pain
from lymphadenopathy, hemorrhage, ulcerations, bone and joint pain.
Diagnosis:
Clinical features, blood smear showing immature cells, bone marrow aspirate.
Complications:
Hemorrhages, infections.
Treatment:
Supportive (transfusions, antibiotics), chemotherapy, corticosteroids, bone marrow transplant.
Nursing Care:
Infection prevention, emotional support, education, transfusion monitoring, skin care, pain
management.

Wilms' Tumor
Definition:
Malignant embryonic kidney tumor, common in infants and toddlers, usually unilateral. 
Clinical Features:
Abdominal mass, low-grade fever, hematuria, later anemia, cachexia.
Diagnosis:
IV pyelogram, CT scan, kidney function tests.
Treatment:
Surgical  nephrectomy  (avoid  palpation  preoperatively  to  prevent  metastasis),  radiotherapy,
chemotherapy.
Nursing Care:
Avoid abdominal palpation, skin care during therapy, parental anxiety reduction. 
Hodgkin’s Disease
Definition:
Malignant lymphoma affecting lymph nodes, more common in boys under 8 years. 
Clinical Manifestations:
Painless  cervical  lymphadenopathy,  weight  loss,  anorexia,  fever,  cough,  splenomegaly,
hepatomegaly, elevated ESR.
Diagnosis:
Biopsy of lymph node.
Treatment:
Surgery, radiation, chemotherapy depending on disease stage.
Stages:
I-IV based on extent; treatment intensity and prognosis vary accordingly.
Follow-Up:
Lifelong monitoring for recurrence and late effects, rehabilitation services.
