Unit Thirteen: Hematological Disorders
Beta Thalassemia
Definition:
Inherited blood disorder with defective hemoglobin synthesis causing anemia. 
Pathophysiology:
Mutations in alpha or beta globin genes impair hemoglobin production.
Symptoms:
Fatigue, pallor, bone deformities (facial), abdominal swelling, dark urine.
Complications:
Iron  overload  (from  transfusions),  infections,  bone  deformities,  splenomegaly,  growth  delay,
heart failure.
Diagnosis:
Blood tests showing microcytic anemia, hemoglobin electrophoresis.
Treatment:
Regular  transfusions,  iron  chelation  (deferoxamine),  possible  splenectomy,  bone  marrow
transplant.
Nursing:
Monitor anemia, transfusion reactions, iron overload, support nutrition, infection
prevention, family education. 
Sickle Cell Anemia
Definition:
Autosomal recessive disorder causing sickle-shaped RBCs that obstruct blood flow leading to
ischemia and organ damage. 
Symptoms:
Pain crises (vaso-occlusive), anemia, jaundice, infections, growth delay, ulcers, stroke risk.
14
Diagnosis:
CBC, sickle cell test, hemoglobin electrophoresis.
Treatment:
Pain management, hydration, infection prevention (antibiotics, vaccines), transfusions, folic acid,
monitoring complications.
Nursing:
Pain assessment and management, hydration, infection monitoring, education on crisis
prevention, psychosocial support. 
Hemophilia A
Definition:
X-linked recessive bleeding disorder due to factor VIII deficiency. 
Symptoms:
Prolonged bleeding, bruising, joint/muscle hemorrhage.
Treatment:
Factor VIII replacement, RICE protocol for bleeding, avoid aspirin.
Nursing:
Bleeding prevention, injury avoidance, pain control, family education, psychological
support. 
Idiopathic Thrombocytopenic Purpura (ITP)
Definition:
Immune-mediated platelet destruction causing bleeding tendency. 
Symptoms:
Purpura, petechiae, bruising, mucosal bleeding, risk of intracranial hemorrhage.
Diagnosis:
CBC showing thrombocytopenia, exclusion of other causes.
15
Treatment:
Steroids, IV immunoglobulin, platelet transfusions, splenectomy in refractory cases.
Nursing:
Bleeding and injury prevention, infection control, patient and family education, monitoring
coagulation. 
Child Rights and Child Health
Definitions:
Right:  Legal entitlement enforceable by law, ensuring care, protection, and provision by
the government.
Need:  A desire or necessity that is not legally enforceable.
Child Health:
Physical, mental, emotional, and social well-being from infancy through adolescence.
Children’s Rights Law:
Ensures every child’s right to survive, grow, develop fully, and participate in decisions affecting
them.
Types of Rights:
Survival (life, shelter, food)
Developmental (education, health services, moral guidance)
Protection (from harm, discrimination)
Participation (in decision-making)
Responsibilities:
Provide adequate food, clothing, health care, education, and protection.
Ten Key Child Rights:
Include right to safety, identity, shelter, health care, love, education, play, protection from labor
and discrimination, and upbringing in peace and tolerance.•

Best Practices for Child Protection:
Adherence to non-discrimination, legal monitoring, abuse hotlines, malnutrition prevention,
emergency care, counseling, nutrition, reporting mechanisms. 
Impact of COVID-19:
Pandemic affects children physically and psychologically; recommended measures include safe
outdoor activities respecting health protocols, alternative educational support for those with
