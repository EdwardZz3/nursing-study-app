UNIT SIX: E.N.T AND RESPIRATORY
PROBLEMS
Upper Respiratory Disorders
1. Common Cold:
Caused by:  rhinovirus, coronavirus
Symptoms:  clear to purulent nasal discharge, cough, feeding difficulty in infants
Diagnosis:  clinical; differentiate from allergic rhinitis and early measles/pertussis
Treatment:  saline nasal drops, paracetamol, fluids; antibiotics not indicated
2. Acute Nasopharyngitis:
Description:  Viral infection with potential bacterial second stage
Symptoms:  vary by age; fever, sneezing, nasal congestion, cough, irritability
Complications:  sinusitis, otitis media, pneumonia
Treatment:  nasal decongestants, bed rest, hydration, steam inhalation, nasal irrigation
Nursing:  isolation, nutrition, comfort care, prevention of cross infection
3. Croup:
Description:  Viral or bacterial inflammation of larynx and trachea causing airway obstruction
Symptoms:  respiratory distress, stridor, hoarseness, cough, fever
Treatment:  humidified oxygen, nebulized epinephrine, antibiotics if bacterial, monitor vitals
Nursing:  airway clearance, anxiety reduction, fever management, emergency preparedness
4. Tonsillitis:
Description:  Infection/inflammation of tonsils, mostly viral, some bacterial
Symptoms:  fever, sore throat, swollen lymph nodes, pharyngeal exudate
Complications:  otitis media, abscesses
Treatment:  symptomatic, antibiotics if bacterial, tonsillectomy if chronic
Nursing:  pre/postoperative  care  including  bleeding  monitoring,  diet,  pain  management,  parent

5. Otitis Media:
Description:  Middle ear infection, often secondary to upper respiratory infection
Symptoms:  earache, fever, irritability, hearing loss
Treatment:  antibiotics, analgesics, possible myringotomy
Nursing:  monitor for complications, hygiene, hydration, hearing evaluation
Lower Respiratory Disorders
1. Bronchial Asthma:
Description:  Chronic inflammatory airway disease causing bronchospasm and airflow obstruction
Risk factors:  allergens, irritants, infections, weather, exercise, emotions, food additives
Symptoms:  wheezing, dyspnea, cough, cyanosis
Treatment:  bronchodilators  (epinephrine,  aminophylline),  corticosteroids,  oxygen,  hydration,
antibiotics if infection
Nursing:  monitor respiratory status, avoid triggers, educate family on management
2. Pneumonia:
Description:  Lung  parenchyma  inflammation  caused  by  viruses,  bacteria,  aspiration,  or  hospital-
acquired pathogens
Classifications:  bronchopneumonia, lobar pneumonia, interstitial pneumonia
Symptoms:  fever, cough, rapid breathing, chest retractions, grunting, inability to drink
Diagnosis:  chest X-ray, blood cultures
Treatment:  antibiotics (gentamicin, benzylpenicillin for <2 months; amoxicillin for older children),
oxygen, supportive care, physiotherapy
Nursing:  hydration, rest, monitoring for complications, education on follow-up•
